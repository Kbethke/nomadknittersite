FAQ

Your family is coding this game on a boat!?

How does that work?

Q. What is MoM built in?

A. We are using Python as the main language, and kivy as our sound and graphics library.

Q. Kivy - what?

A. Oh, kivy is this AWESOME cross-platform (Android, OSX, Windows, and iOS) openGL wrapper so you can make games that include high-performance animation and 3D graphics (we happen to prefer 2D for MoM) all while working in Python!

Q. How do you guys get internet while on a boat?

A. Pretty much everywhere you can now get good to great mobile connectivity and then swap in local sim cards that are priced appropriately for local markets. On top of that we often work out of cafes and bars, and then a good amount of times we can pick up WIFI from Ad Astra with our WIFI boosing attenae.

Q. How long have you been working on this?

A. We started December 2016, so about 18 months so far.

Q. When will it be released?

A. We are going to take our time with Million on Mars. After 20+ years of making games with various schedules, Erik is taking all of his experiences and lessons and we are building a hand-crafted artisan game we hope folks will enjoy for years. Q. “That’s nice. But seriously, when will you release? A. “We hope to have the first playable Alpha in available in early 2019.”

Q. Where will you release it?

A. We do not know yet, first from this site. Likely Steam down the road.

Q. What platforms will you support?

A. We are targetting desktop OSX, Linux and PC. The game is UX intensive and does not lend its design to a mobile platform, although we do plan to support tablets in the future. No current plans for consoles.

Q. What sort of community features do you have planned?

A. The community will be the central design force for Million on Mars, we cannot be domain experts on all of the science and engineering needed to re-build human civilization on Mars. For that reason we are building a system that uses a wiki + a reputation system similar to Stack Overflow and the players themselves will be able to edit and even create new game resources, crafting recipes and even improve on the art and quest system.

Q. How many people are working on this?

A. Four people: Max (art and code), Kyle (design, audio and code), Kaiwen (QA and localization) and Erik (rest)

Q. Who made the art and animations?

A. Max Bethke created all of the art and animations
